1. Executive Summary & The Crisis

This section outlines the foundational context of the water hyacinth (Eichhornia crassipes) invasion at Pashan Lake. Users will find qualitative insights into the ecological decay caused by unchecked urban runoff from the Ramnadi basin. This serves to establish the urgency of the problem before delving into empirical data.

Pashan Lake, a vital man-made reservoir in Pune built in the British era, is facing an unprecedented ecological collapse. The uncontrolled proliferation of water hyacinth has covered nearly 85% of the water surface during peak seasons.

This invasive weed forms a thick mat that blocks sunlight, halts photosynthesis for submerged native flora, and drastically reduces Dissolved Oxygen (DO). The root cause is severe eutrophication—nutrient loading (phosphorus and nitrogen) primarily from untreated sewage and greywater entering via the Ramnadi river, which acts as a conduit for waste from surrounding developing areas under PMC and PCMC jurisdictions.

Key Implications

  • Biodiversity Loss: Severe drop in migratory bird sightings (historically a haven for them) and massive fish kills due to hypoxia.
  • Vector Breeding: The stagnant, choked waters provide ideal breeding grounds for mosquitoes, posing a public health risk to surrounding neighborhoods.
  • Economic Drain: PMC spends crores of rupees annually on superficial mechanical removal without addressing the nutrient inflow, resulting in cyclical regrowth within weeks.

3. Official Position & Mitigation Framework

This is the core of the position paper. It delineates actionable recommendations into immediate, rapid responses and sustainable, long-term engineering solutions. It is designed to be directly presented to municipal engineers and policymakers as a clear, phased approach rather than a single monolithic task.

Immediate Containment (0-6 Months)

🚜

Strategic Mechanical Harvesting

Move away from manual perimeter cleaning. Deploy specialized aquatic weed harvesters. Crucially, extracted biomass must be transported out of the catchment area immediately to prevent nutrient leaching back into the soil/water.

🚧

Floating Boom Barriers

Install physical floating booms at the Ramnadi inlet. This prevents new hyacinth mats flowing in from upstream areas and traps solid macroscopic waste, isolating the lake for internal cleanup.

🪲

Biocontrol Initiation

Introduce host-specific natural enemies like the Neochetina weevil. While slow, starting this phase immediately ensures sustained pressure on the plant's reproductive cycle.

Addressing the Root Cause (6 Months - 3 Years)

1. Ramnadi Interception & STP Upgrades

The lake is currently a sink for raw sewage. PMC/PCMC must map all illegal outfalls along Ramnadi.

  • Construct decentralized Sewage Treatment Plants (STPs) focusing on tertiary treatment (Phosphorus removal).
  • Implement strict phytoremediation buffers along the river banks before water hits the lake.

2. Constructed Floating Wetlands (CFWs)

Replace the invasive hyacinth with managed native vegetation.

  • Deploy CFWs using native species (e.g., Canna, Vetiver) that absorb nutrients but do not form impenetrable mats.
  • These provide aesthetic value, bird habitats, and actively clean the water without dying and sinking to create sludge.

3. Desilting and Lake Bed Restoration

Years of dead hyacinth have created a thick layer of nutrient-rich benthic sludge. Even if water inflow is purified, this sludge acts as an internal nutrient load.

Require scientific dredging of the lake bed during the dry season, ensuring safe disposal of the toxic sludge, to reset the lake's baseline depth and nutrient profile.

Inputs for Municipal Corporations (PMC & PCMC)

To effectively implement the physical solutions, administrative and policy shifts are required from the local bodies. Here is the drafted advisory for presentation:

  1. Joint Task Force: Pashan Lake lies at the periphery of PMC, but its feeder (Ramnadi) passes through areas impacting both PMC and PCMC limits. Establish a unified Ramnadi-Pashan Restoration Committee to prevent jurisdiction-shifting.
  2. Open Data Auditing: Mandate the use of public satellite data (as detailed in the next section) to track contractor performance. Payments for "lake cleaning" must be tied to objective, remote-sensed reduction in vegetation index, not just truckloads of wet biomass measured at the site.
  3. Budget Reallocation: Shift 60% of the recurring annual "weed removal" budget towards capital expenditure for decentralized STPs and interceptor lines along the Ramnadi.

Acquiring Imagery: Sentinel & Landsat

To monitor Pashan Lake continuously, we use multispectral optical imagery. These satellites pass over Pune every few days, providing free data.

Platform Options:
  • Copernicus Data Space Ecosystem (Browser): Best for Sentinel-2. Offers 10m spatial resolution. Excellent for small lakes like Pashan.
  • USGS EarthExplorer: The standard for Landsat 8/9 data (30m resolution). Good for historical data going back decades.
  • Google Earth Engine (GEE): Best for programmatic analysis (JavaScript/Python API) to generate automated time-series without downloading massive files.

Action: Create a free account on Copernicus Data Space or Google Earth Engine.

Defining the Lake Boundaries

You must isolate Pashan Lake from the surrounding urban vegetation to prevent skewed data.

  • Locate Pashan Lake coordinates: approx. 18.536° N, 73.785° E.
  • Use GIS software (like QGIS - free/open source) or the drawing tools in Earth Engine.
  • Draw a polygon strictly within the high-water mark of the lake.
  • Save this geometry as a GeoJSON or Shapefile. This acts as your mask; only data inside this shape will be processed.

Spectral Analysis: NDVI vs NDWI

We don't just look at photos; we use math on light bands (Near Infrared - NIR, Red, and Green) to classify pixels as either "Water" or "Vegetation (Hyacinth)".

NDVI (Vegetation)

NDVI = (NIR - Red) / (NIR + Red)

Values > 0.2 indicate vegetation floating on the surface.

NDWI (Water)

NDWI = (Green - NIR) / (Green + NIR)

Positive values indicate open, clear water surfaces.

By calculating the percentage of pixels inside your lake polygon with high NDVI, you obtain an exact, objective percentage of hyacinth coverage for that specific date.

Converting Data to Auditing Action

How to use this methodology to hold authorities accountable:

  • Baseline Creation: Generate a chart of NDVI coverage for the past 5 years. This proves the cyclical nature of the problem and the failure of current methods.
  • Contract Auditing: When PMC claims "100% clearance" on a specific date, pull the Sentinel-2 image for that week. Run the NDVI script. Include the satellite output in your report to either verify or refute the contractor's claim.
  • Publishing: Compile the methodology, Python/GEE scripts, and results into a White Paper under the Pune Knowledge Cluster (PKC) banner for peer review and press release.
